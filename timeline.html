Key dates and milestones.

From the 2023 annexation through the upcoming City Council vote, here is the public record of what's happened and what's coming next. Use the "Add to calendar" links to put the upcoming dates on your phone or computer calendar so you don't miss them.

  1. 2023

    Ord. 2739 — Gardner annexes the ~300-acre parcel

    The City of Gardner adopts Ordinance 2739, annexing the agricultural parcel at 191st & S. Clare Rd. into the city limits. The annexation boundary excludes residential parcels on three sides.

    Read the annexation story →
  2. 2024

    Ord. 2769 & 2775 — adjacent ag parcels annexed

    Two further annexation ordinances bring additional agricultural parcels into the city limits, again leaving adjacent residential homes in unincorporated Johnson County.

  3. 2025

    Ord. 24-16 — additional ag parcel annexed

    Another agricultural parcel in the area is brought inside the city. The annexation footprint now covers most of the contiguous agricultural land at the 191st & S. Clare intersection.

  4. March 2026

    KCUR reports on Kansas data center land deals

    Public reporting at KCUR / NPR documents that two out-of-state data center developers are negotiating to buy more than 1,000 acres of Kansas land — often under non-disclosure agreements.

    Read the KCUR story →
  5. April 2026

    Beale Infrastructure publicly announces Gardner project

    Beale Infrastructure publishes its Gardner project page. Residents and the broader community begin to learn the details of what is proposed for the parcel.

    Beale Infrastructure project page → Read our project summary →
  6. May 13, 2026 · 6:00 p.m.

    Public Meeting #1 (Gardner)

    First City-hosted public meeting on the proposal. Attend in person, listen, and ask questions. Verify time and location with the City Clerk.

    Add to calendar → How to prepare →
  7. May 15, 2026 · 6:00 p.m.

    Public Meeting #2 (Gardner)

    Second City-hosted public meeting. Same agenda topic; a second opportunity to attend if you can't make the May 13 meeting.

    Add to calendar →
  8. May 26, 2026 · 7:00 p.m.

    Gardner Planning Commission Hearing

    The Planning Commission reviews the rezoning application and makes a formal recommendation to the City Council. Public comment is invited and becomes part of the official record.

    This is the meeting where in-person testimony has the most direct impact.

    Add to calendar → How to speak at the hearing →
  9. June 9, 2026 (approx.)

    Protest petition deadline (K.S.A. 12-757)

    Approximate deadline to file a valid protest petition under K.S.A. 12-757 — 14 days after the Planning Commission hearing concludes. If owners of 20% or more of property within 1,000 feet of the rezoning area sign a valid petition, the City Council vote requires a 3/4 supermajority instead of a simple majority. Verify the exact deadline with the City Clerk.

    Add reminder → How protest petitions work →
  10. June 15, 2026 · 7:00 p.m.

    Gardner City Council Vote

    City Council votes on the rezoning application. Public comment is taken before the vote. This is the final decision-making meeting.

    Add to calendar → Email your council members →
